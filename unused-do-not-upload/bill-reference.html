Bill Reference for Questionnaires

See below for a complete glossary of every bill mentioned in the questionnaires.

The Baltimore Sun Editorial Board developed questionnaires for candidates for state and local races to be used both in the endorsement process and in The Sun’s online voters guide. We will make endorsements in the Democratic and Republican primaries for governor, the Democratic primary for Attorney General and for selected General Assembly and local races.

The editorial board will determine endorsements based on candidates’ answers to the questionnaires, additional research and interviews. They will be announced in June.

2011 - HB1213/SB994 - Alcohol tax increase
Added a 3 percent sales tax surcharge to alcoholic beverages.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2011 - HB470/SB167 - Dream Act
Allowed immigrants without legal status in the United States to receive in-state tuition at Maryland’s public colleges and universities under certain circumstances.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2011 - HB302/SB172 - Requiring governor to respond to parole recommendations for those serving life sentences
Stipulated that Parole Commission recommendations would go into effect automatically after 180 days unless the governor intervened.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2011 special session - HB1/SB1
Congressional redistricting after 2010 census – Established new congressional district boundaries to reflect shifts in population.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2012 - HB438/SB241 - Gay marriage
Overturned Maryland’s law defining marriage as the union of a man and a woman.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2012 - HB445/SB236 - Limiting residential subdivisions served by septic systems
Required counties to establish “growth tiers” to encourage denser development and discourage large residential subdivisions served by septic systems rather than public water and sewer.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2012 - HB446/SB240 - Flush tax increase
Doubled the Chesapeake Bay Restoration Fund fee from $2.50 to $5 a month, with the proceeds dedicated to upgrading wastewater treatment plants.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2012 - HB987/SB614 - Requiring local governments to enact stormwater remediation fees
Mandated that counties enact fees to pay for stormwater remediation projects necessary to meet the Environmental Protection Agency’s “pollution diet” for the Chesapeake Bay.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2012 - HB1801/SB1301 - Shifting some teacher pension costs to the counties
Determined that county governments and Baltimore City would be responsible for a portion of the funding for teachers, an expense that had previously been paid exclusively by the state government.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2012 - HB1802/SB1302 - Increase in income tax rates on high earners/increased tobacco taxes
Raised individual income tax rates on the top 20 percent of earners, increased the tax on tobacco products other than cigarettes and cigars.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2012 - HB1/SB1 - Casino expansion and authorization of table games
Authorized a voter referendum on whether the state should allow a sixth casino (to be built in Prince George’s County) and whether table games should be legalized.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2013 - HB295/SB276 - Death penalty repeal
Changed the severest criminal penalty in Maryland law from capital punishment to life without the possibility of parole.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2013 - HB860/SB743
Baltimore City Schools construction and renovation – Established a new financing mechanism that will allow $1.1 billion in new construction and renovation to Baltimore City schools in the coming years.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2013 - HB226/SB275 - Offshore wind
Authorized a surcharge of up to $1.50 per month on an average residential customer’s electric bill to help finance a proposed off-shore wind farm near Ocean City.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2013 - HB294/SB281- Gun control -- handgun licensing, assault weapons ban, etc.
Banned assault rifles and high-capacity ammunition magazines; tightened laws related to the prohibition on gun ownership by those who have been treated for mental illness; and established a licensing system for handgun purchasers.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2013 - HB1515/SB1054 - Gas tax increase to fund transportation projects
Imposed a new sales tax on gas, to be phased in over three years, and indexed the existing per-gallon tax to inflation, with a total expected impact of 20 cents a gallon by 2016; dedicated the proceeds to road construction and mass transit.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2014 - HB295/SB331 - Minimum wage increase
Increased Maryland’s minimum wage to $10.10 an hour by 2018.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2014 - SB364- Marijuana decriminalization
Eliminated criminal penalties for the possession of small amounts (less than 10 grams) of marijuana.
  • Senate Bill
2014 - HB1232/SB973 - Bail reform
Would have replaced bail commissioner hearings with a computerized risk-assessment tool to determine whether criminal defendants should be released or held after arrest.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2014 - HB1265/SB212 - Transgender anti-discrimination bill
Banned discrimination in employment, housing and public accommodations for transgender people.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2014 - HB1298/SB395 - Combined reporting method for corporate income tax
Would have changed the way corporate taxes are calculated in an effort to prevent multi-state corporations from hiding profits in states with lower or no corporate income tax.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2014 - HB418/SB691 - Public campaign financing
Would have established a system of public campaign financing for races for attorney general, comptroller and the General Assembly.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
2014 - HB1213 - Congressional redistricting commission
Would have established a commission comprised of equal numbers of Democrats and Republicans and a smaller number of independent members to re-draw the state’s congressional district boundaries after each census.
  • House Bill
2014 - HB384/SB290 - Hybrid school board for Baltimore County
Changed the composition of the Baltimore County school board to include seven elected members and four appointed members.
  • House Bill
  • Senate Bill
